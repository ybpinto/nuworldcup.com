Registration for Northwestern's largest student run athletic tournamanent begins here, Monday February 1st at 12pm!

For the first time we'll also be having a Women's World Cup consisting of 8 teams, in addition to the World Cup with 32 teams!

Important Registration Information

  • Registration is on a first come first served basis.
  • Each team must be comprised of 10 to 16 players.
  • For NU World Cup, each team can have a maximum of two players who are, or have been, club players. For NU Women's World Cup, each team can have a maximum of three players who are, or have been, club players. Female players can play in both NU World Cup and the NU Women's World Cup.
  • Registration is $10 per player that will be collected at the Captain's Meeting. This covers organizing costs, lunch on the day of the tournament, t-shirts, bottles and other merchandise!
  • Each team is required to raise 100$ to benefit Gonzo Soccer, a Chicago based non-profit organization that aims to empower young women through the shared love of soccer. Failure to raise this amount may result in expulsion from the tournament.
  • Once 32 teams have registered, we will start a waitlist for all subsequent registrants. We have had teams initially on the waitlist play in the tournament in previous years, so if you're on the wait list, you're not out of luck!
  • Registration will be here, on NUWorldCup.com, on Monday, February 1st, 12pm!

Since its inception in 2010, NU World Cup has been bringing the Northwestern community together through the shared love for the game of soccer. Working towards an even more inclusive and diverse NU family, we organize events throughout the year to help our philanthropy and promote our main event - the Northwestern World Cup.

Throughout the year, our six committees put together events such as the FIFA and Futsal tournaments, Human Foosball and fundraisers for our philanthropy.

The Northwestern World Cup is a 3-day event, based on the format of the actual FIFA World Cup, organized at the Lakeside Field. With 32 teams and over 500 participating players, Northwestern World Cup is the largest student run athletic event on Northwesternâ€™s campus.

Initially starting off as ISA World Cup in 2010, NU World Cup has been an independent organization since 2012. At the end of just our first year as an organization, we received the 2013 Wildcat Excellence Award for Outstanding Undergraduate Student Organization.

As we continue to grow and expand each year, we strive for an even more united Northwestern community. None of this would be possible without our committee members, sponsors and members of the Northwestern community who have all given NU World Cup the love and support to transform us into the organization we are today!
